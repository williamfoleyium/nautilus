Sanctions Disclosures


What are the new restrictions?
The new restrictions prohibit sales of CHF and EUR-denominated transferable securities issued after 12 April 2022, or investment funds providing exposure to those securities, to any Russian and Belarusian persons. specifically, these are as follows:
- Council Regulation (EU) No 833/2014 of 31 July 2014 concerning restrictive measures in view of Russia's actions destabilising the situation in Ukraine, as amended (the "Regulation 833/2014"), in particular by Council Regulation (EU) 2022/328 of February 2022 amending Regulation No8333/2014;
- Council Regulation (EC) No 765/2006 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Belarus and the involvement of Belarus in the Russian aggression against Ukraine, as amended (the "Regulation 765/2006"), by Council Regulation (EU) 2022/398 of 9 March 2022 amending Regulation (EC) No 765/2006
- The Swiss Ordinance of 4 March 2022 on Measures in Connection with the Situation in Ukraine (SR 946.231.116.72)
-The Swiss Ordinance of 16 March 2022 on Measures with Respect to Belarus (SR 946.231.116.9)
What are "Russian and Belarusian persons"?
"Russian/Belarusian person" are defined to include any Russian/Belarusian national or natural person residing in Russia/Belarus or any legal person, entity or body established in Russia/Belarus. The restrictions also apply to any legal person whose ultimate beneficial owner is a Russian/Belarusian person but does not apply to nationals of EU or natural persons having a temporary or permanent residence permit in EU member State. In the case of Swiss restrictions, the prohibition does not apply to nationals of Switzerland or an EEA member state or natural persons, having a temporary or permanent residence permit in Switzerland and/or EEA member state.
What are "transferable securities"?
"Transferable security" is defines in Article 1(f) of Regulation 833/2014 (and equivalent Belarus regulation) as the following classes of securities, including in the form of crypto assets, which are negotiable on the capital market, excluding instruments of payment:
- Shares in companies and other securities equivalent to shares in companies, partnerships or other entities, and depositary receipts in respect of shares;
- Bonds or other forms of securitised debt, including depositary receipts in respect of such securities;
- Any other securities giving the right to acquire or sell any such transferable securities or giving rise to cash settlement determined by reference to transferable securities.
Certain derivatives are also classed are transferable securities, including options, futures, forwards or warrants irrespective of how they are traded (OTC or on exchange) where they give the right to acquire or sell transferable securities referred to in (i) or (II).
